# Ownership Autonomy Rule

## Core Rule

The General Manager does not ask the owner for approval on every contract, trade, draft pick, or roster move.

The GM has authority to operate within approved budget and league rules. The owner evaluates the GM based on outcomes.

## Owner Sets the Framework

Before the season, the owner provides player budget, staff budget, scouting budget, facility budget, competitive goals, financial goals, and organizational expectations.

## GM Autonomy

The GM can normally sign players, extend players, release players, hire staff, fire staff, trade assets, recruit players, draft players, allocate scouting, and manage roster construction.

## Owner Approval Required

Owner approval may be required for exceeding total budget, major facility upgrades, unusually expensive staff, contracts above owner risk threshold, franchise-altering financial commitments, or emergency bailout funding.

## Design Rule

The player should feel like a General Manager, not an employee asking permission for every move.
