# Ownership Autonomy Rule

## Core Rule

The General Manager does not ask the owner for approval on every contract, trade, draft pick, or roster move. The General Manager has authority to operate within the approved budget and league rules. The owner evaluates the GM based on outcomes.

## Owner Sets the Framework

Before the season, the owner provides:

- Player budget
- Staff budget
- Scouting budget
- Facility budget
- Competitive goals
- Financial goals
- Organizational expectations

Inside that framework, the GM controls hockey operations.

## GM Autonomy

The GM can normally sign, extend, and release players; hire and fire staff; trade assets; recruit and draft players; allocate scouting; and manage roster construction.

## Owner Approval Required

Owner approval may be required for exceeding total budget, major facility upgrades, unusually expensive staff, contracts above owner risk threshold, franchise-altering financial decisions, or emergency bailout funding.

## Accountability

The owner should not block every bad move. Bad decisions should damage owner trust, owner confidence, GM reputation, job security, fan opinion, and media perception.

## Autonomy Levels

- Low Autonomy — new GM or low trust. Owner watches closely.
- Normal Autonomy — owner sets budgets. GM manages hockey operations.
- High Autonomy — owner rarely interferes.
- Full Hockey Control — elite GM with long-term success earns near-total hockey authority.

## Design Rule

The player should feel like a General Manager, not an employee asking permission for every move. Owners influence direction. General Managers make decisions. History judges the results.
