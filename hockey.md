# Hockey Bible
## Contract Framework v1

## Purpose

The contract framework must support junior rules now and professional rules later. Contracts are promises, relationships, risks, and career decisions.

## Version 1 Required Contract Types

- Junior player agreement
- Staff contract
- GM contract
- Scout contract
- Coach contract

## Junior Player Agreements

May include term, stipend or salary, education package, housing support, billet arrangement, playing role promise, development promise, and release/transfer conditions.

## Professional Contract Framework

Future rulebooks must support annual salary, term, signing bonus, performance bonuses, roster bonuses, two-way salary, no-trade clause, no-move clause, modified no-trade clause, team option, player option, buyout terms, retained salary, contract termination, arbitration eligibility, restricted free agency, unrestricted free agency, qualifying offers, offer sheets, entry-level contracts, and veteran minimums.

## GM Contracts

GM contracts include title, salary, term, owner expectations, bonuses, job security, extension eligibility, firing penalties, and reputation impact.

## Autonomy Rule

The GM does not need owner approval for normal contracts within approved budget and league rules. The owner judges outcomes later.

## Owner Approval Required

Exceeding budget, contracts above risk threshold, major staff spending, and franchise-altering commitments.
